Competitive Battlecards — Architectural Sensor Faucets

Sales-ready cards for the commercial A&D specification fight. Every card is written to the dual-truth rule — T&S reliability and A&D design/spec-workflow credibility — and laddered to Qualified Specification Intent (QSI). Audience: architects, interior designers, MEP/plumbing engineers, and spec writers. Never consumer/DIY.

Truth 1 — Reliability Built In

~80-year U.S. commercial faucet manufacturer. Serviceable AC/DC module, temp-mixing valve, vandal-resistant 0.5 GPM platform, full cert stack on every SKU.

Truth 2 — Designed to be Specified

A true architectural finish range — Brushed Brass & Polished Graphite among five finishes — on a commercial-duty sensor platform. Holden Brushed Brass is the flagship aesthetic moment.

Read first: honesty guardrails

Say these wrong and you hand the specifier a reason to doubt everything else

Four things not to overclaim

  • Do NOT say competitors "have no BIM." Sloan, Zurn, Chicago, Moen, and American Standard all publish Revit/BIM (verified 2026-07-09). Today WaveCrest's BIM is the weaker asset — live on BIMobject as "WaveCrest Touchless Faucets," but buried on-site and token-based. Compete on design + duty + finish + reliability, not on BIM availability.
  • Buy-American is real but narrow. T&S manufactures in the U.S. (Travelers Rest, SC) — a credible Made-in-USA story — but formal Buy American Act certification currently sits on a limited set of SKUs. Confirm per-SKU before citing BAA in a federal/GSA spec.
  • The Holden Brushed Brass Revit family is not yet downloadable. If a specifier asks for the flagship's Revit token, we can't hand it over today (P0 fix in flight). Don't promise it live; offer the spec sheet and the BIMobject collection.
  • Warranty is parity, not an edge. 3-year limited — standard for sensor electronics. Don't lead with it.

Sloan

The commercial restroom incumbent — the unspoken default spec.
AI SOV 0.86Primary head-to-head
Their pitch

"The most specified commercial restroom brand." Water efficiency, hygiene, and the complete BASYS / Optima / AER-DEC system — faucet, flush, soap, and sink coordinated as one. EcoPower self-generating power as the sustainability proof point.

Honest strengths
  • Default incumbent — often specced by habit; huge installed base and facilities-team familiarity.
  • Complete restroom system, not just a faucet — hard to displace on integrated jobs.
  • Strongest AI/answer-engine visibility in the category (0.86).
  • Does publish BIM — native BIM Models page + Optima families on BIMobject.
Where they're vulnerable
  • Utilitarian aesthetic — finish range is engineered, not architectural; no true brushed-brass design story.
  • Positions on efficiency/hygiene, rarely on design — cedes the design-forward, guest-facing restroom.
  • System lock-in cuts both ways: heavy where the job only needs a well-specified faucet.
Our dual-truth differentiators
  • Design finishes on a commercial-duty platform. Brushed Brass & Polished Graphite on a vandal-resistant, 0.5 GPM, ADA/NSF sensor faucet — a combination Sloan's utility range doesn't offer. Proof: Holden ECW-3156-BB; five-finish range.
  • Specialist reliability + serviceability. ~80-year commercial faucet maker; serviceable AC/DC module and temp-mixing valve standard. Proof: full cert stack on all 31 SKUs.
  • Focus is the feature. When the spec is the faucet — not the whole flush system — a purpose-built sensor line specs cleaner.
Objection handling
"Sloan is the standard — why switch?""For back-of-house, it's a fine default. For guest-facing and design-forward restrooms where finish coordinates to the palette, Sloan's range is utilitarian. WaveCrest gives you Brushed Brass or Polished Graphite on the same commercial-duty, 0.5 GPM, ADA/NSF platform."
"Sloan has EcoPower / a full system.""Right — for a fully integrated flush+faucet system, that's their strength. Where the specification calls for a discrete deck-mount sensor faucet with a specified architectural finish, that's exactly where WaveCrest is purpose-built."
Landmine to set

"How important is finish and aesthetic coordination in your guest-facing restrooms?" — surfaces the design gap early.

Landmine to defuse

"Does WaveCrest have a full restroom system like BASYS?" → "WaveCrest is a focused sensor-faucet line, not a flush system — which is the advantage when the faucet is what's being specified for its design and duty." Never claim Sloan lacks BIM.

Win / loss themes

We win on design-forward, hospitality, and higher-ed front-of-house where finish is specified. We lose on pure integrated-system retrofits and habitual back-of-house specs. Favors them: facilities-driven, system-first buyers. Favors us: architect/ID-driven, finish-first buyers.

Zurn / Elkay

The spec-ecosystem benchmark. Zurn is the real sensor-faucet threat; Elkay is the sister brand (sinks, bottle fillers).
Zurn 0.86Elkay 0.06
Their pitch

Zurn: connected, engineered water management — Cumberland & Serio sensor faucets inside the "Zurn One" coordinated systems, with best-in-class specifier tooling. Elkay: sinks, ezH2O bottle fillers, and commercial water delivery.

  • Engineering-first, not design-first — sensor faucet aesthetics are limited.
  • Interior designers rarely reach for Zurn on finish; it reads "utility," not "architectural."
  • Elkay's sensor-faucet answer-engine presence is negligible (0.06) — the family is not a design-faucet force.
Our dual-truth differentiators
  • Design range Zurn's sensor line can't match. If the palette calls for Brushed Brass or Polished Graphite, WaveCrest specs it; Zurn's sensor finishes don't go there. Proof: Stafford 3159 & Holden 3156 full BB+PG range.
  • We're on BIMobject too. The WaveCrest Touchless Faucets collection is live — so "are you even in the ecosystem?" has a yes. Proof: bimobject.com WaveCrest collection.
  • Reliability heritage + U.S. service. 80-year specialist with serviceable modules.
Objection handling
"Zurn's BIM and spec tools are better.""Their spec tooling is strong — we're actively expanding ours, and the WaveCrest collection is already on BIMobject. Where WaveCrest wins outright is the finish and design range on a commercial-duty sensor platform. Zurn's sensor line can't deliver Brushed Brass or Polished Graphite."
"We standardize on Zurn One for coordination.""Makes sense for fully coordinated MEP packages. On the faucet line item specifically, where design finish is called out, WaveCrest is the design-forward, commercial-duty choice that still meets every cert."
Landmine to set

"Which of your sensor faucets can you actually get in the finish the designer specified?" — Zurn's honest answer is narrow.

Landmine to defuse

"Is WaveCrest even on BIMobject / in the spec ecosystem?" → "Yes — the WaveCrest Touchless Faucets collection is live on BIMobject, and our spec-authority build is underway." Don't out-claim Zurn on ecosystem breadth — acknowledge and pivot to design.

Win / loss themes

We win when the interior designer/architect owns the finish decision. We lose to Zurn on engineer-led, coordination-first specs that value the connected system. Favors them: MEP-driven; favors us: design-driven.

WaveCrest — our own cheat-sheet

Lead with this. The dual-truth position, the open lanes, and the honest edges.

Our one-line position

The commercial sensor faucet you don't have to design around. Architectural finishes — including Brushed Brass and Polished Graphite — on a vandal-resistant, 0.5 GPM, fully certified commercial platform, from an 80-year U.S. manufacturer.
